One of the common types of cybercrime today is online job fraud, which mainly targets students and job seekers. In this type of scam, fraudsters create fake job opportunities and advertise them through emails, social media, or job portals. These offers usually promise high salaries, flexible work, or easy selection processes to attract people quickly.

In many cases, the victim is asked to pay a small amount as a registration fee, training charge, or security deposit. Since the offer appears genuine, many people trust it and make the payment. However, once the money is transferred, the fraudsters either stop responding or completely disappear. In some situations, they also collect personal details such as identity proof or bank information, which can later be misused.

These scams are increasing because people often do not verify the authenticity of the company or offer. The urgency created by fraudsters also makes victims act without thinking carefully.

This case study shows that awareness and caution are very important while dealing with online opportunities. It is always safer to verify the source, avoid making payments, and stay alert while sharing personal information on digital platforms.
